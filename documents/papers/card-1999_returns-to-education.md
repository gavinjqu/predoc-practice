# Card 1999 — Returns to Education

**Identification:** Within-family vs. between-family fixed effects to net out unobserved family-level confounders; IV exploiting structural innovations (e.g., pupil-teacher ratio, schooling reforms) as exogenous shifters of schooling.

**Methodology:** Survey of IV, family-FE, and (where available) RCT estimates of the return to schooling.

**Key insight:** IV estimates of the return to education are typically *higher* than OLS — a puzzle, since classical measurement error / ability bias predicts the opposite. Possible explanation: selection bias on the subpopulations whose schooling decisions are moved by the structural innovation used as the instrument (i.e., LATE on compliers with high marginal returns).

**Relevance to my research:** Canonical paper on IV and the return to education. Baseline reference for any returns-to-schooling work and the natural starting point for the human-capital vs. signaling debate.
