# Spence 1973 — Signaling via Education

**Identification:** Theoretical separating equilibrium under asymmetric information about worker productivity. No empirical identification — the paper proves existence of equilibria where education sorts workers by type.

**Methodology:** Game-theoretic model of a labor market with two worker types and a costly signal (education).

**Key insight:** Applicants will invest in a signal if the offered wage increase exceeds the cost of acquiring the signal. A separating equilibrium requires that high-productivity workers find it strictly cheaper or easier to signal than low-productivity workers — this single-crossing condition is what makes the signal informative.

**Relevance to my research:** Education is not necessarily making workers more productive; it may simply act as a prerequisite that proves they are high-productivity. The canonical counter-story to the human-capital interpretation of Card 1999's returns to schooling, and a foundational reading for any labor-econ work on education.
