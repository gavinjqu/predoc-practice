# Dix 2025 — Combining Complements

**Identification:** Uses micro data on combination cancer therapies to measure externalities and spillovers from one firm's innovation onto the value of complementary innovations. Identification comes from variation in the timing and combinations of approved therapies, structured through a dynamic model of firm R&D choices.

**Methodology:** Dynamic structural model of innovation decisions plus reduced-form evidence from cancer-treatment combination data.

**Key insight:** Combination innovation is ubiquitous in software and pharmaceutical industries, and often results in marginal private cost exceeding the marginal *social* cost — leading to under-production of socially valuable innovation when private firms don't internalize complementarities.

**Relevance to my research:** Industrial-organization angle of the market for health, and a modern empirical test of the Arrow 1962 under-investment claim. Useful template for combining structural modeling with policy-relevant questions in healthcare innovation.
