# Arrow 1962 — Innovation and Resource Allocation

**Identification:** Theoretical. Compares monopoly and competitive equilibrium incentives to invest in invention.

**Methodology:** Theoretical welfare analysis of R&D under different market structures.

**Key insight:** Both monopoly and competitive equilibrium result in under-production of innovation: resources should be devoted to invention until the expected marginal social benefit equals the marginal social cost, but private appropriability is incomplete. Hence government, university, and private (philanthropic) funding intervene to push investment toward the socially optimal level.

**Relevance to my research:** Allocation of resources and IO topics around healthcare and medical innovation are increasingly studied as new AI tools enter drug discovery and diagnostics. This is the canonical theoretical anchor for any empirical paper on R&D under-investment (including Dix 2025).
