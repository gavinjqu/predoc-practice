# Arrow 1963 — Normative Analysis of Medical Care

**Identification:** Theoretical. Applies the two fundamental welfare theorems to the medical-care market and derives where they fail.

**Methodology:** Corollary of the optimality theorems: (1) if competitive equilibrium exists, it is Pareto-optimal; (2) if there are no increasing returns in production, every Pareto-optimal state is a competitive equilibrium. Then asks how supply and demand of medical care depart from these conditions.

**Key insight:** Supply and demand of medical care differ from other markets because uncertainty (over incidence of illness and effectiveness of treatment) plays a pivotal role in healthcare preferences and decisions, creating systematic market failures (moral hazard, adverse selection, agency).

**Relevance to my research:** Healthcare-market angle of labor economics; the foundational paper for almost everything in health econ. Provides the welfare framework underlying current debates on insurance design and pharmaceutical innovation.
