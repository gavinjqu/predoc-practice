# Sadoff 2024 — College Summer School

**Identification:** Randomized assignment ($Z$) of summer-school scholarships at a community college. Because actual take-up ($D$) is endogenous (the student chooses), the paper uses IV-for-LATE: $Z$ instruments for $D$, recovering the causal effect on *compliers* (students who attended summer school *because* of the scholarship).

**Methodology:** Field experiment with scholarship offers; reduced-form ITT plus IV for LATE.

**Key insight:** Summer-school scholarships increase community-college transfer rates to four-year universities and raise GPAs. The setting is particularly stark because over 80% of community-college students *intend* to transfer to a four-year school, yet only about a quarter actually do — leaving substantial room for a marginal intervention to move outcomes.

**Relevance to my research:** Demonstrates the practicality and robustness of experimental design and its intersection with IV methodology — a clean template for any RCT where compliance is partial. Also directly relevant to the returns-to-education and human-capital-vs-signaling debate.
