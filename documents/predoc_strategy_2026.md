## Top-level moves that raise Predoc/RA odds fastest

1. **Optimize for letters + research signal (not just skill accumulation).**
   The highest-leverage outcome is: *a credible economist (or frequent coauthor of economists) can write “this person can do research”*. Everything else supports that.

2. **Turn your skills into visible research outputs.**
   “I solved X problems / read Y book” is weak; “I replicated Z paper + produced a clean repo + memo + robustness checks” is strong.

3. **Specialize just enough to be legible.**
   Keep breadth, but pick 1–2 “lanes” (e.g., labor/public/IO, or macro/finance) so your portfolio and networking look coherent.

4. **Get close to real research workflows.**
   Version control, reproducible pipelines, data documentation, writing short memos, and responding to feedback quickly are exactly what predocs do.

5. **Apply broadly and early, with a refined packet.**
   A great packet + wide net beats perfect preparation + narrow net.

6. **Math: prioritize what matters for PhD readiness and RA productivity.**
   Linear algebra + probability/stat inference + optimization basics + real analysis (as planned). Don’t let less-relevant detours crowd out these.

7. **Programming: depth in one stack beats shallow in many.**
   For econ RA: Python/R + SQL + reproducibility (git, testing, environments) + data engineering basics (files, APIs, ETL) + plotting/reporting.

## Pillar F — GRE (maximize score efficiently)

Treat GRE like an optimization problem:

* **2–3 full-length practice tests/month** (timed, realistic conditions)
* Error log with categories: careless, concept gap, time management
* Drill only the categories that move score fastest (often quant speed/accuracy)

If “perfect score” is the goal, your bottleneck is usually **speed + unforced errors**, not content.
